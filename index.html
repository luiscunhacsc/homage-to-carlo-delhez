The Scientific Mind

Delhez applied the rigorous logic of a physicist to the world of software development. In 1994, he received his doctorate from the Eindhoven University of Technology, delivering a thesis titled "The azimuthally varying field racetrack microtron".

While his academic career explored complex physics, his passion for computing centered on Sinclair platforms. Even as technological paradigms shifted, Delhez remained an anchor for the ZX81 community, building tools that defied the hardware limitations of the era.

Emulator Masterpieces

Bridging the 8-bit past to modern architectures through high-performance simulation.

XTender & XTender II

MS-DOS / WINDOWS

A definitive emulation suite running ZX80, ZX81, and Aszmic ROMs. XTender set a standard by introducing direct audio loading/saving via soundcards, a virtual tape interface, multiple keyboard layouts, and an advanced "TV emulator" to faithfully reproduce original video behaviors.

XTricator

SINCLAIR QL

Announced in 1992, XTricator allowed the 16-bit Sinclair QL to flawlessly run ZX81 software. Praised in reviews like the International QL Report, the emulator saw continued development, with version 1.75 reaching Update Magazine in 1994.

Software Ecosystem

Tools, interpreters, and utilities that expanded what was possible on Sinclair hardware.

01

Coral Basic Interpreter

Developed in 1987 for the 16K ZX81 (Terminal Software). It provided an updated BASIC environment, introduced new commands, and featured a revolutionary full-entry editor, allowing users to bypass the tedious Sinclair keyword-token entry system. Crucially, it introduced advanced features like procedures and inline assembly, making it arguably more powerful and flexible than even the lauded Sinclair QL SuperBasic.

02

ZX-Assembler-2

A massive revision of Artic Computing's original assembler in 1991. Delhez added the much needed ORG directive and various profound optimizations. Most importantly, it dramatically lowered the barrier to entry for learning Z80 Assembly: by allowing users to return to their state before a crash, it removed the fear of losing unsaved work due to simple typos.

03

ZX81Tape & Custom OS

Announced in 1995 via Update Magazine, the ZX81Tape utility allowed users to convert cassette-based ZX81 programs into mountable MS-DOS files. Moreover, he created a custom Operating System that allowed ZX81 users to load and save programs directly to and from the PC file system, finally achieving the rock-solid reliability that the Sinclair Microdrives only dreamed of.

The Standard of Truth

CLCKFREQ

Among his native utilities was the ZX81 Processor Test (1991), commonly circulated as CLCKFREQ. It was meant to stress-test 2K ZX81 hardware configurations.

"Decades after its original release, Delhez's utility remains the definitive benchmark. In 2022, the iOS emulator 'ZX81' specifically cited scoring 100% on CLCKFREQ as proof of its cycle-exact accuracy."
